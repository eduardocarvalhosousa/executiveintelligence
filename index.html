Gulf Nations Align Behind US Strikes — Iran Braces for Escalation

Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and other Gulf states have quietly signalled support for US military strikes targeting Iranian nuclear and missile infrastructure, according to Semafor's sources. The alignment marks a significant diplomatic shift, with Gulf monarchies previously reluctant to openly back military action against Tehran.

Iran has responded by putting its air defence systems on high alert and deploying naval assets in the Strait of Hormuz. Oil markets are pricing in the disruption risk — Brent crude jumped 2.3% overnight, adding to year-to-date gains driven by supply uncertainty.

The White House has not confirmed the diplomatic outreach but senior officials publicly stated that "all options remain on the table." European allies are reportedly urging restraint ahead of their own diplomatic window.

"Gulf partners have communicated, in private channels, their readiness to support a limited strike operation." — Semafor diplomatic source

Private Markets Face Valuation Reckoning as Rate Plateau Bites

The Daily Upside's deep-dive this morning flags a structural stress building across private equity and private credit funds: mark-to-market pressures are intensifying as the Fed's extended plateau has eroded the exit windows that GPs had pencilled in for 2025–26. Deal flow is down 34% year-on-year, while the denominator effect is keeping LP commitments soft.

Secondary markets are filling the gap — but at steep discounts. The analysis notes that several mid-market PE funds are sitting on vintage-2021 portfolios where median EV/EBITDA multiples have compressed from 14x to under 9x, with no clear liquidity path before 2027.

For CEOs with PE-backed businesses, this creates a dual pressure: operational performance expectations remain high even as the exit horizon extends, and covenant pressure on overleveraged portfolio companies is quietly rising.

"We are in the longest and most uncomfortable hold cycle since 2009. The question is no longer when the exit comes — it's whether the exit looks anything like the entry." — Daily Upside, quoting senior LP

Lead Story AI Infrastructure · API
Anthropic Launches Remote Computer Use API — Agents Can Now Operate Live Systems

Anthropic has released its Remote Computer Use API, enabling Claude to operate desktop applications, browsers, and file systems on behalf of enterprise users via a secure cloud sandbox. Unlike local computer-use implementations, the remote version runs in Anthropic's managed environment — meaning companies can deploy agentic workflows without exposing their own infrastructure.

The practical implications are significant: customer support agents that can file tickets, refund orders, and update records without human intervention; data analysts who can run code, pull reports, and format outputs autonomously; and compliance workflows that can gather evidence, log decisions, and generate audit trails end-to-end.

The Rundown AI notes that this positions Anthropic directly against Microsoft's Copilot Studio and Salesforce's Agentforce — but with a model capability advantage that remains the key differentiator. Pricing is on a per-task basis, with enterprise contracts expected to anchor the early revenue.

"This is the moment where AI stops being a co-pilot and starts being the pilot. The question for every CEO is: which workflows are you ready to hand over?" — Rundown AI
